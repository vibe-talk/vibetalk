Anonymous Chat Glossary

Definitions of common terms used in anonymous chatting and online communication.

Jump to:

A B C D E G H I M P R S T V W

A

Anonymous Chat
A form of online communication where participants can converse without revealing their real identities. Users typically interact using pseudonyms and without sharing personal information like names, locations, or contact details.
Active Listening
A communication technique involving fully concentrating on what someone is saying, understanding their message, and responding thoughtfully. In text-based chat, this means referencing what the other person has shared and asking follow-up questions. See our communication skills guide.
ASL
An acronym for "Age, Sex, Location"—a common question in older chat rooms asking for basic demographic information. This practice is now considered outdated and potentially invasive in many communities.

B

Block
A feature that allows you to prevent a specific user from contacting you or viewing your profile. Blocking is an essential safety tool that should be used when someone behaves inappropriately.
Bot
An automated program that simulates human conversation. In anonymous chat, bots are often used to send spam, advertise services, or collect information. Signs of bots include generic messages, quick requests for personal info, and unnatural response patterns.

C

Catfishing
When someone creates a fake online identity to deceive others, typically by using false photos and fabricated personal information. Catfishers may do this for emotional manipulation, financial scams, or other deceptive purposes.
Chat Room
A virtual space where multiple users can communicate simultaneously through text. Traditional chat rooms are being replaced by one-on-one matching platforms, but the term is still used broadly for online chat spaces.
Cyberbullying
The use of digital devices, sites, or apps to bully someone through messages, posts, or other content. In chat contexts, this includes harassment, threats, spreading rumors, or deliberately excluding someone.

D

Doxxing
The malicious practice of researching and publicly revealing someone's private or identifying information (address, phone number, workplace, etc.) without their consent. This is a serious violation of privacy and often illegal.

E

End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)
A method of secure communication where only the communicating users can read the messages. The messages are encrypted on the sender's device and can only be decrypted by the recipient's device, preventing even the platform provider from accessing the content.
Etiquette (Online)
The accepted code of conduct for online communication, including being respectful, not spamming, protecting others' privacy, and following platform rules. See our etiquette guide for detailed practices.

G

Ghosting
Abruptly ending communication with someone without explanation, essentially disappearing from the conversation. While sometimes necessary for safety, ghosting without cause can be considered poor etiquette.

H

Harassment
Unwanted behavior that disturbs or upsets someone, including persistent unwanted contact, threats, intimidation, or offensive language. Online harassment is often illegal and should be reported to platforms and, in serious cases, authorities.

I

IP Address
Internet Protocol address—a unique numerical identifier assigned to each device connected to the internet. IP addresses can reveal approximate geographic location and are typically logged by chat platforms. VPNs can mask your IP address.
Interest Matching
A feature on some chat platforms that pairs users based on shared interests or topics they've selected. This can lead to more relevant and engaging conversations compared to purely random matching.

M

Moderation
The process of monitoring and controlling content on a platform to ensure it meets community guidelines. Moderation can be done by humans, automated systems, or a combination. Good moderation is a key indicator of a safe platform.
Matching Algorithm
A system used by platforms to pair users for conversation based on various criteria such as interests, language, location, or random selection. The quality of matching affects the user experience significantly.

P

Peer-to-Peer (P2P)
A network architecture where participants communicate directly with each other without going through a central server. In chat contexts, P2P connections can offer more privacy but may also present security challenges.
Phishing
A fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information (passwords, credit card numbers, etc.) by disguising as a trustworthy entity. In chat, phishing often involves suspicious links or requests for account verification.
Pseudonym
A fictitious name used instead of one's real name. Using pseudonyms is standard practice in anonymous chat to protect identity. Also called a "username," "handle," or "screen name."
Privacy Policy
A legal document describing how a platform collects, uses, and protects user data. Reading the privacy policy before using a chat platform helps you understand what information is being collected about you.

R

Random Chat
A type of chat service that pairs users randomly for conversation. Unlike interest-based matching, random chat connects you with whoever is available at the moment, creating unpredictable and diverse interactions.
Report
The act of notifying a platform about a user who is violating rules or behaving inappropriately. Reports are reviewed by moderators who take action against offending accounts. Reporting helps keep communities safe.

S

Skip
A feature in random chat that allows you to immediately end a conversation and be matched with a new person. Skipping is common and shouldn't be taken personally—there are many reasons people skip.
Spam
Unsolicited, repetitive, or irrelevant messages, often sent in bulk. In chat contexts, spam usually involves advertising, promoting websites, or sending the same message to many users. Spam accounts should be reported.
SSL/TLS
Secure Sockets Layer / Transport Layer Security—security protocols that encrypt data transmitted between your browser and a website. Look for "https://" in URLs, indicating SSL/TLS protection of your connection.

T

Text Chat
Communication through typed messages rather than voice or video. Text chat offers more anonymity and accessibility compared to video chat, as it doesn't require a camera or reveal your appearance.
Terms of Service (ToS)
The rules and guidelines you agree to when using a platform. ToS typically outline acceptable behavior, prohibited content, and the platform's rights and responsibilities. Violating ToS can result in account suspension or banning.
Trolling
Deliberately posting provocative, offensive, or off-topic content to upset others or disrupt conversations. Trolls seek negative reactions and are best handled by not engaging and reporting their behavior.

V

Video Chat
Real-time communication using video through webcams or phone cameras. Video chat provides a more personal connection but sacrifices some anonymity and requires more bandwidth than text chat.
VPN (Virtual Private Network)
A service that encrypts your internet connection and masks your IP address by routing your traffic through servers in other locations. VPNs provide additional privacy when using chat platforms, especially on public WiFi.
Voice Chat
Audio-only communication in real-time, a middle ground between text and video chat. Voice chat offers more personal connection than text while maintaining some visual anonymity.

W

WebRTC
Web Real-Time Communication—technology that enables browser-to-browser video, voice, and data sharing without requiring plugins. Many video chat platforms use WebRTC. Note that WebRTC can reveal your IP address even with a VPN.
